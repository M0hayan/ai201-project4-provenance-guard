**Detection Signals**

The system will use two independent detection signals to determine whether submitted text is more likely to be human-written or AI-generated.

**Signal 1 – LLM-Based Classification (Groq)**

This signal sends the submitted text to the Groq API using the `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` model. The model analyzes the writing as a whole, considering factors such as coherence, tone, sentence structure, repetition, and overall writing style. It returns a confidence score between **0.0 and 1.0**, where values closer to **1.0** indicate a higher likelihood that the text was AI-generated.

**Signal 2 – Stylometric Heuristics**

This signal measures statistical characteristics of the writing without considering its meaning. The heuristics include sentence length variation, vocabulary diversity (type-token ratio), punctuation density, and average sentence complexity. These measurements are normalized into a score between **0.0 and 1.0**, where higher values indicate that the text exhibits characteristics more commonly associated with AI-generated writing.

**Combining the Signals**

The final confidence score is calculated by taking a weighted average of the two signals. The LLM classifier will contribute **60%** of the final score because it evaluates the writing holistically, while the stylometric heuristics contribute **40%** because they provide an objective structural analysis. This combined score is then used to determine the final classification and transparency label.

---

**Uncertainty Representation**

The confidence score represents how strongly the combined signals support the final classification rather than claiming certainty about who actually wrote the text.

A confidence score around **0.60** means the signals lean toward one conclusion but do not strongly agree. This result is treated as uncertain enough that the system avoids making a definitive claim.

The system will use the following thresholds:

- **0.00 – 0.39:** Likely Human-Written
- **0.40 – 0.69:** Uncertain
- **0.70 – 1.00:** Likely AI-Generated

These thresholds help reduce overconfident classifications when the two signals disagree or produce only weak evidence.

---

**Transparency Label Design**

The transparency label translates the technical classification into plain language that readers can easily understand.

**High-Confidence AI**

> **Likely AI-Generated**
>
> This content appears to have been generated by AI. The system has high confidence in this assessment.

**High-Confidence Human**

> **Likely Human-Written**
>
> This content appears to have been written by a person. The system has high confidence in this assessment.

**Uncertain**

> **Attribution Uncertain**
>
> The system could not confidently determine whether this content was written by a person or generated by AI. Readers should interpret this result with caution.

---

**Appeals Workflow**

Any creator whose content has been analyzed may submit an appeal if they believe the classification is incorrect.

An appeal must include:

- The content ID.
- A written explanation describing why the creator believes the classification is inaccurate.

When an appeal is submitted, the system will:

- Record the creator's explanation.
- Link the appeal to the original attribution decision.
- Update the content status from **Classified** to **Under Review**.
- Add a new entry to the audit log with the appeal details and timestamp.

When a human reviewer opens the appeal queue, they will see:

- The original submitted text.
- The original AI/human classification.
- The confidence score.
- Individual outputs from both detection signals.
- The transparency label that was shown to readers.
- The creator's appeal explanation.
- The submission and appeal timestamps.
- The current status ("Under Review").

---

**Anticipated Edge Cases**

**Poetry with repetitive language**

Poems often intentionally repeat words, phrases, and sentence structures for artistic effect. The stylometric heuristics may interpret this consistency as AI-like writing, even though the work is entirely human-created.

**Highly edited AI-generated content**

A creator may generate a draft with AI and then substantially rewrite it by hand. The LLM classifier may view the final version as human-written while the stylometric heuristics still detect patterns associated with AI writing, resulting in an uncertain classification.

**Technical documentation or academic writing**

Formal documents frequently use consistent sentence structures, specialized vocabulary, and predictable formatting. These characteristics may resemble AI-generated writing even when the document was written entirely by a person.

**Very short submissions**

A short poem, paragraph, or social media post may not provide enough information for either detection signal to make a reliable assessment. With limited text, the system will likely return a lower confidence score and classify the submission as uncertain.

**## Architecture**
Submission Flow

The submission flow begins when a creator sends text to the POST /submit endpoint. The system validates the request, analyzes the text using both the LLM classifier and stylometric heuristics, combines the results into a single confidence score, generates a reader-friendly transparency label, records the decision in the audit log, and returns the final classification, confidence score, and label to the client.

Appeal Flow

The appeal flow allows a creator to contest an attribution decision by submitting a reason through the POST /appeal endpoint. The system updates the content's status to Under Review, records the appeal alongside the original decision in the audit log, and returns a confirmation response so the appeal can later be reviewed by a human moderator.
